VI.4 · Capital markets and insurance platforms — trading, post-trade, asset management, P&C, life

Two adjacent BFSI worlds, often run by the same enterprise, with very different software estates. Capital markets is the world of trading, risk and post-trade — Murex, Calypso, Summit, Aladdin, Charles River, Bloomberg AIM, FIS Front Arena, ION, Adenza, Broadridge. Insurance is the world of policy admin, claims, actuarial and reinsurance — Guidewire, Duck Creek, Sapiens, EIS, Majesco, FINEOS, Prophet, AXIS. This topic gives you fluent vocabulary across both, the regulators that shape them, and the design choices a techno-functional leader makes when modernising either.

0. Primer — capital markets and insurance, said briefly Basic

Primer — read this even if you think you know it

Capital markets and insurance share a common parent — they manage long-tail financial promises — but their software estates evolved separately and look very different. A trading floor runs on millisecond-grade platforms talking to exchanges and clearing houses. An insurance back-office runs on multi-year policy and claims systems talking to actuarial models, reinsurers and regulators. Both are now under heavy modernisation pressure for similar reasons: cloud, AI, and a regulator stack that has rewritten itself in the last three years.

0.1 IT-side anchor — two factories with different cycle times

From what you already know — your laptop

A factory that builds smartphones runs on minute-by-minute discipline; a factory that builds aircraft runs on month-by-month discipline. Both are factories, both have inventory and quality control and supply chains, but you don’t buy one set of software for both. Capital markets is the smartphone factory: high frequency, low latency, every millisecond visible. Insurance is the aircraft factory: 30-year contracts, actuarial reserves, regulator-blessed assumptions. Same company can own both; you still buy two different stacks.

0.2 BFSI-side anchor — the trade and the policy

From what you already know — your bank account

When a hedge fund buys £10m of a UK gilt, the trade passes through an Order Management System (OMS), an Execution Management System (EMS), an exchange or RFQ venue, a clearing house, a custodian, and dozens of risk and reporting steps inside the bank — all in seconds. When you buy a 25-year term life policy, the application passes through a quote engine, an underwriting platform, a policy admin system, an actuarial reserving engine, a reinsurance platform and a claims system over decades. Both stories live inside the same multi-line BFSI group.

0.3 Capital markets in five product blocks

  • Equities — cash equities, ETFs, single-stock derivatives.
  • Fixed income — government and corporate bonds, repo, money markets.
  • FX and rates — spot, forwards, swaps, options.
  • Credit and structured — credit derivatives, securitised products, structured notes.
  • Commodities — energy, metals, agricultural; physical and financial.

0.4 Insurance in three lines of business

  • Property & Casualty (P&C, also ‘General Insurance’) — auto, home, commercial, marine, aviation, cyber.
  • Life & Annuities (L&A) — term life, whole life, universal life, annuities, savings products with insurance wrappers.
  • Health and reinsurance — health insurance, group benefits, and the reinsurers (Munich Re, Swiss Re, Hannover Re, Lloyd’s syndicates) that back the primary insurers.

1. Why both estates are under heavy modernisation Basic

Why are both estates being modernised right now?

  1. Regulator pressure on resilience. EU DORA covers both; UK FCA Operational Resilience and PRA SS2/21 outsourcing apply equally; Solvency II (insurance) and FRTB / Basel 3.1 (banks) require new data depth.
  2. T+1 settlement. US, Canada, Mexico moved to T+1 on 28 May 2024; UK and EU are coordinating a T+1 migration on 11 October 2027; India already T+1, moving to T+0. Post-trade systems must be faster.
  3. Insurance accounting upheaval. IFRS 17 took effect 1 January 2023 globally (US uses LDTI under US GAAP). It rewrote every insurer’s reserving and reporting architecture.
  4. Cloud-native modernisation. Murex MX.3 on cloud, Calypso (now Adenza) on cloud, Aladdin on Azure, Guidewire Cloud, Duck Creek OnDemand all push the industry off on-prem.
  5. AI on long-tail data. Insurance claims and underwriting are perfect AI use cases; capital-markets risk and surveillance are increasingly ML-driven.
  6. Crypto and tokenised assets. BlackRock BUIDL, JPM Onyx, HSBC Orion, Goldman GS DAP, Citi Token Services — capital-markets infrastructure is preparing for tokenised cash and securities.

3. How both estates actually work — vendors, regimes, data flows Intermediate

3.1 Capital markets — front office (OMS, EMS)

  • Order Management System (OMS) — the buy-side / sell-side system that holds positions, cash, and orders. Charles River IMS (SS&C-State Street), BlackRock Aladdin, Bloomberg AIM, SimCorp Dimension, FactSet Portfolio Lifecycle Management, SS&C Eze Eclipse, Enfusion.
  • Execution Management System (EMS) — the trader-facing system for execution. FlexTrade, Bloomberg EMSX, ION Fidessa, Virtu Triton, TS Imagine.
  • FIX protocol — Financial Information eXchange; the universal language between OMS, EMS, venues and brokers.
  • Algo trading and SOR — Smart Order Routing chooses the best venue; algo strategies (VWAP, TWAP, POV, IS) implemented in C++/Java with tight latency budgets.

3.3 Capital markets — post-trade and clearing

  • Central Counterparties (CCPs) — LCH (LSEG), ICE Clear, CME Clearing, Eurex Clearing, DTCC FICC / NSCC, JSCC, NSCCL / ICCL (India).
  • CSDs (Central Securities Depositories) — DTCC (US), Euroclear and Clearstream (EU), CDSL / NSDL (India), HKSCC.
  • Custodians — State Street, BNY Mellon, JPM, Citi, Northern Trust, BNP Paribas, HSBC; provide safekeeping, corporate actions, settlement.
  • Trade repositories for derivatives (EMIR, Dodd-Frank, MAS / ASIC equivalents).
  • SWIFT messaging — securities messages (MT 5xx, ISO 20022 sese / semt / seev).

3.4 Capital-markets reporting regimes you must know

  • MiFID II / MiFIR (EU + UK) — best execution, transaction reporting, transparency; MiFIR refit in EU 2024 raises bar.
  • EMIR / EMIR REFIT (EU) — derivatives trade reporting; EMIR REFIT applied 29 Apr 2024.
  • SFTR (EU/UK) — Securities Financing Transactions Regulation.
  • Dodd-Frank Title VII (US) — swaps reporting to SDRs (DTCC, ICE).
  • CAT (Consolidated Audit Trail, US) — every order, route, modification, execution.
  • FRTB (Fundamental Review of the Trading Book) — Basel III.1 trading-book capital framework; in force in EU 1 Jan 2025 (revised 2025), UK 1 Jan 2026, US delayed.
  • SEC Rule 17a-4 — broker-dealer record-keeping; WORM storage.

3.6 Insurance — accounting and reporting regimes

  • IFRS 17 Insurance Contracts — in effect from 1 January 2023 globally (ex-US); rewrote reserving, contractual service margin, transition methods. Engines like FIS Prophet Enterprise, Moody’s AXIS, RNA Analytics R3S, SAS for IFRS 17.
  • US LDTI (Long-Duration Targeted Improvements) — ASU 2018-12; in effect since 2023 for SEC filers.
  • Solvency II (EU/UK) — risk-based capital regime; UK Solvency UK reforms (effective 2024–25); EU Solvency II Review adopted 2024.
  • RBC (US Risk-Based Capital), BCAR (Best’s), SAM (South Africa), ICS (IAIS Insurance Capital Standard) from 2026.
  • India IRDAI Risk-Based Capital transition under preparation; current regime is factor-based.
  • NAIC Model Audit Rule, SOX for insurers, EIOPA Guidelines in EU.

4. Region by region and vendor consolidation Intermediate

4.1 United States — capital markets and insurance

  • SEC, FINRA, CFTC regulate broker-dealers, swap dealers, exchanges; Reg NMS, Reg ATS, Reg SCI shape exchange and ATS technology.
  • T+1 since 28 May 2024; T+0 voluntary for some products.
  • Insurance is state-regulated via NAIC + 50 state insurance departments; federal exposure via FIO and Treasury.
  • Climate disclosure — SEC climate rule stayed pending litigation (2024); California SB 253 / 261 effective 2026.

4.2 United Kingdom and Eurozone

  • FCA / PRA / Bank of England for banks; PRA for insurers; Solvency UK reforms reduce risk-margin and ease capital for insurers (2024–25).
  • EU MiFID II / MiFIR Refit (2024), EMIR REFIT (Apr 2024), EU Solvency II Review (2024).
  • EU CSDR (penalties for settlement fails); EU AI Act for life pricing and credit-life products.
  • UK + EU T+1 target 11 October 2027.
  • EU FiDA (Financial Data Access) — Open Insurance proposal in trilogue 2024–25.

4.3 India and APAC

  • SEBI regulates capital markets; T+1 fully in force since Jan 2023; T+0 same-day settlement live in beta from Mar 2024.
  • NSE / BSE exchanges; NSCCL / ICCL clearing; NSDL / CDSL depositories.
  • IRDAI regulates insurance; Bima Sugam insurance marketplace launching 2025; risk-based capital framework consultation ongoing.
  • Singapore MAS for both; Hong Kong SFC + HKEX + HKIA; JFSA + JPX; ASIC + APRA + ASX.

4.4 The vendor consolidation that matters

  • Nasdaq + Adenza (2023) brought Calypso and AxiomSL under Nasdaq alongside SMARTS surveillance.
  • LSEG + Refinitiv (2021) created the second-biggest financial-data platform after Bloomberg.
  • SS&C + Advent / Eze / Black Diamond consolidated wealth and asset-management technology.
  • FIS + Worldpay split (2024); FIS kept the capital-markets and banking businesses.
  • Broadridge + Itiviti (2021) brought front-office trading workflow into Broadridge.
  • ICE bought Black Knight (2023) for mortgage; capital-markets effect via MERSCORP and ICE Mortgage data.
  • Insurance: Roper sold ConstructConnect; Sapiens privatised by Advent (2024) consultation; Duck Creek went private with Vista 2023.

5. Advanced — risk engines, surveillance, AI in insurance, tokenisation Advanced

5.1 Risk technology — the engines behind the dashboards

  • Market risk — VaR, ES (Expected Shortfall under FRTB), historical simulation, Monte Carlo; engines inside Murex, Calypso, FIS, Numerix, in-house C++/Python grids.
  • Credit risk — counterparty CVA / DVA / FVA; xVA desks run grids the size of small banks; Quantifi, Numerix, Murex are common.
  • Liquidity risk — Basel LCR / NSFR; intraday liquidity via SmartStream, Bottomline, Vermeg.
  • Operational risk — RCSA, scenario analysis; Archer, OpenPages, MetricStream, ServiceNow IRM.
  • Insurance risk — Solvency II internal model or standard formula; ORSA; actuarial valuation engines.

5.2 Surveillance, e-comms and conduct risk

  • Trade surveillance — Nasdaq SMARTS, NICE Actimize Markets, Eventus Validus, b-next, Trillium / TLX.
  • Communications surveillance — Behavox, Smarsh, Global Relay, NICE Comms, Veritone Voice; massive growth post the 2021–24 SEC and CFTC fines on off-channel comms (>USD 3B in fines across the industry).
  • Best execution analytics — Big xyt, LiquidMetrix, IHS Markit / S&P TCA.

5.3 Insurance — claims AI and digital underwriting

  • FNOL automation — First Notice of Loss via mobile, with vision LLMs assessing damage for motor and home claims.
  • Straight-through claims — low-complexity claims paid in minutes (Lemonade is the well-known fintech example; incumbents are catching up).
  • Risk pricing — telematics for motor, wearables for life, IoT for property; ML pricing models replacing GLM-only books.
  • Fraud detection — Shift Technology, FRISS, FICO Insurance Fraud, in-house ML.
  • Underwriting copilots — Generative AI surfacing relevant clauses, prior claims, market data; speeds up commercial / specialty underwriting (Liberty Mutual, AXA XL public references).

5.4 Tokenised assets and the next capital-markets rail

  • Tokenised money-market funds — BlackRock BUIDL on Ethereum (March 2024) — USD 500M+ AUM by end of 2024; Franklin Templeton OnChain US Government Money Fund.
  • Tokenised repo — Broadridge DLR settling tens of trillions notional cumulatively; JPM Onyx, HSBC Orion, Goldman GS DAP.
  • Project Agorá (BIS, 2024) — multi-central-bank tokenised wholesale settlement.
  • EU MiCA in force since 30 Dec 2024; UK Digital Securities Sandbox operational from 2024; Singapore MAS Project Guardian tokenisation pilots; India RBI wholesale CBDC pilot for G-Sec settlement.
  • Implication for platforms — Murex, Calypso, Aladdin, Broadridge are all building tokenised-asset workflows so that the capital-markets estate can route tokens like any other instrument.

6.3 Build vs buy — capital markets vs insurance

  • Capital markets — buy the trading and risk platform; build the differentiating algo and analytics layer; integrate via FIX / ISO 20022 / files.
  • Insurance — buy the policy, claims and billing platforms; build the digital channels, underwriting analytics and AI claims layer.
  • Both estates: standardise on cloud (Azure or AWS), OpenTelemetry, GitOps, OPA — see IV.1.

7. Worked examples — five real BFSI capital-markets and insurance journeys Intermediate

Worked example — BlackRock Aladdin — the buy-side platform of record
  1. Aladdin started as BlackRock’s internal portfolio and risk platform; opened to external clients in 1999.
  2. 200+ external clients with USD 20T+ in assets under platform; Allianz, MEAG, Prudential, AustralianSuper, Mitsubishi UFJ Asset.
  3. Major Azure migration ongoing; eFront integration for private markets; Aladdin Climate for climate-risk analytics.
  4. Concentration-risk discussion: regulators (FSB, ESMA) are watching; some clients now run challenger systems on the most critical controls.
Worked example — ION January 2023 — concentration risk made visible
  1. Lockbit ransomware against ION Cleared Derivatives (Trading Technologies / FIS / SunGard heritage) took down derivatives back-office at dozens of tier-1 banks for over a week.
  2. Trades had to be processed manually; CFTC Commitment of Traders report was delayed for the first time in modern memory.
  3. Lessons: critical-third-party risk in capital markets is real; DORA designations of CTPs were tightened in response.
Worked example — Allianz — Guidewire Cloud at global scale
  1. Allianz is rolling out Guidewire Cloud (PolicyCenter, ClaimCenter, BillingCenter) across European P&C operations under the ‘Allianz Customer Model’ programme.
  2. Implementation partners include EY, Capgemini, Accenture, HCLTech across geographies.
  3. Lessons: tier-1 carrier global rollout is multi-year; configuration discipline and partner ecosystem dominate the success curve.
Worked example — An Indian life insurer on Sapiens ALIS + Prophet for IFRS 17
  1. Mid-tier Indian life insurer runs Sapiens ALIS for policy admin; Prophet Enterprise for actuarial reserving; integrated to Oracle GL.
  2. IFRS 17 implementation 2021–23 with HCLTech / Mastek as integrators; CSM (Contractual Service Margin) tracked from 1 Jan 2023.
  3. IRDAI risk-based-capital transition prep underway 2024–25.
Worked example — Standard Chartered Treasury — modernising on Calypso (Adenza)
  1. SC consolidated treasury and capital-markets risk on Calypso (now Adenza, Nasdaq) over 2018–24; Murex retained for some businesses.
  2. Move to AWS deployment for Calypso; HCLTech is one of the partners for application services.
  3. FRTB readiness completed for EU 1 Jan 2025 go-live; UK 1 Jan 2026 in flight.

8. Questions a leader asks in any capital-markets or insurance review Basic

  1. What instruments / lines of business does each platform cover, and where are the duplicates?
  2. What is our T+1 readiness — UK/EU 11 Oct 2027 — and are post-trade systems ready to settle in 24 hours?
  3. What is our FRTB programme status (EU live 2025, UK 2026), and how is it integrated with our risk platform?
  4. Where are we on IFRS 17 / LDTI run state — is it production-stable or still firefighting close cycles?
  5. What is our Aladdin / Murex / Calypso / ION concentration risk, and is there a credible challenger / exit plan per DORA?
  6. What surveillance coverage do we have on trade and comms, and is e-comms covered to the standard the SEC is fining on?
  7. How are we adopting Guidewire Cloud / Duck Creek OnDemand / Sapiens cloud, and have we tested DR for the SaaS deployment?
  8. What is our claims-AI roadmap and what fraud savings have we measured?
  9. What is our tokenised-assets roadmap — BUIDL-style MMF, repo, secondary trading?
  10. How are we using EU MiCA / UK DSS / Singapore Project Guardian / India CBDC pilots to learn before scaling?
  11. What is our reinsurance ceded-book accounting platform, and is it ready for IFRS 17 PAA / GMM?
  12. Are conduct risk and best-execution analytics running on data the regulator finds defensible?

9. Red flags + topic glossary Basic

Red flag — push back if you hear this
  • ‘Capital markets and insurance can share one core platform.’ — They cannot. Lifecycle, latency and regulators are different. Share data, not core systems.
  • ‘Aladdin / Murex / Calypso is too big to fail.’ — ION January 2023 says otherwise. Plan for a TLPT-level outage.
  • ‘IFRS 17 is finished.’ — Year-1 close is finished. Reserving granularity, transition hangovers and audit fine-tuning are still 2–3 years of work.
  • ‘FRTB is just a model upgrade.’ — It is also a data-quality, sensitivity-collection, and IPV (Independent Price Verification) overhaul.
  • ‘T+1 is just a SLA change.’ — It is a global post-trade re-engineering: matching, FX funding, stock-loan recall, all faster.
  • ‘Surveillance covers our floor.’ — Off-channel comms (WhatsApp, Signal, Telegram) cost the industry >USD 3B in SEC + CFTC fines from 2021–24. Coverage must be device-level, not just approved-channel.
  • ‘Claims AI replaces adjusters.’ — It augments them. Loss-of-control complaints under FCA Consumer Duty, EU AI Act high-risk for life pricing — keep a human in the loop.
  • ‘Tokenisation is a sandbox toy.’ — BUIDL crossed USD 500M AUM in 9 months; Broadridge DLR settles real repo. The integration question is now ‘when’, not ‘if’.
  • ‘Reinsurance is the reinsurer’s problem.’ — Ceded-book accounting and bordereaux quality are the cedent’s problem; bad data costs basis points every quarter.
  • ‘Solvency II is settled in the EU.’ — The 2024 Solvency II Review and UK Solvency UK reforms rewrite parts of the regime; engines and reports need updating in 2025–26.
